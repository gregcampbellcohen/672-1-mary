About Kirkwall

Kirkwall is the largest city of Orkney, a cluster of islands ten miles off the coast of Scotland. In the ninth century, Orkney was annexed by Norway, and in the fifteenth century by Scotland. The islands are the site of some of Europe's oldest intact ruins. During the winter holidays, the residents of Kirkwall organize themselves into two mobs -- the 'Uppies' (those on the northern half of the city) and 'Doonies' (those from the South) -- to play the Kirkwall Ba Game, a Scottish predecessor to rugby. The objective of the game is to carry an ornamental ball to the other side of the city by any means necessary. There are no formal rules to the game, and while there is universally observed etiquette, it is not uncommon for players to suffer cracked ribs or broken legs. I would like to observe this tradition in person.
